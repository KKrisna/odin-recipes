Chicken Katsu

How to Make Chicken Katsu

This recipe will show you how to make Chicken Katsu made with an authentic recipe of ingredients. This Japanese style fried chicken is crunchy and juicy in each and every bite. This recipe always brings me back to when I visited Tokyo for the first time and tried an authentic chicken katsu dish from a small mom and pop shop.

Ingredients

For the chicken:

  • 8 skinless, boneless chicken thighs (about 6 ounces each)
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs, lightly beaten with 3 tablespoons of water
  • 3 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • 3 cups vegetable oil, divided
  • Tonkatsu sauce, for serving
  • Steamed sushi rice, for serving

Steps

  1. Lightly pound the chicken thighs 1/2 inch-thick and season with salt.
  2. Put the flour, eggs, and panko in three separate pie plates and season each one lightly with salt.
  3. Dredge 1 piece of chicken in the flour, tapping off the excess..
  4. Dip in the egg, allowing the excess to drip off, then coat the chicken with the panko, pressing to help it adhere.
  5. Transfer the chicken to a wax paperâ€“lined baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining chicken.
  6. Divide the oil between two large skillets and heat until shimmering. Add the chicken and fry over moderately high heat, turning once, until golden and crispy, 3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve the chicken with tonkatsu sauce and steamed rice.
